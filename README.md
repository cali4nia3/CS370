## Connect your learning from throughout this course to the larger field of computer science:

**What do computer scientists do and why does it matter?**

This is quite the broad question, but the core of it boils down to problem solving and creating solutions. Computer scientists build and develop solutions for example, countless every day things we take for granted such as the software behind all our phone applications we use, or the payroll application that tracks our time worked and ensures we are paid appropriately and taxes are withheld and dispersed to the correct entities. It can range from as small as the embedded software within a childs toy responding to input, or as complicated as algorithms behind launching sattelites into space. What computer scientists do matters as they find ways to advance society, achieve the once thought impossible, and streamline previously tedious manual tasks eliminating as much room for error.

**How do I approach a problem as a computer scientist?**

Before jumping in and starting to write code, computer scientists spend countless hours depending on the task at hand fully understanding the complexities about the challenge they are about to tackle. First they must look at all the current components, how it is processed, where it is lacking, failing, and succeeding. Secondly they must understand what their client wants to achieve, their end goals, all the bells and whistles, and create a shared understanding of what is possible and what is outside the realm of possibilty, or does not make sense at the time. When they have a big picture idea only then can they begin to create the diagrams necessary to visualize the problem and identify potential roadblocks or bottlenecks that they can then iron out the details before building the components necessary. As they come together, testing is of the utmost importance and a continued dialogue between developer and client to ensure when the product does come together it not only meets, but exceed expectations.

**What are my ethical responsibilities to the end user and the organization?**

In this day and age we would think our country and the world in general would have made more strides towards equality, but it seems almost on the daily we are taking steps backwards. It's important to ensure that we are creating products that are not engrained with biases and discrimination in the form of sexual, racial, or persons with disabilities. Most recently we see AI algorithms and products being trained on old data that is riddled with biases, particularly when it comes to hiring and filtering candidates for job applications it has been clear that minorities and women have been unfairly overlooked. As we create products we have to have the mindset, and perhaps additional oversight, to ensure that equal access and engrained biases do not find their way into future software. On the other hand, as a software engineer your input does not always hold much value, and if the organization and client responsible for hiring us knowingly wants outdated algorithms or data with potential negative biases, we then have to make the hard decision whether to continue working, or find employment elsewhere.
